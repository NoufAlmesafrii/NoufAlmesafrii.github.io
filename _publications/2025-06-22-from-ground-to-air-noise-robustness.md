This work investigates the robustness of modern deep learning architectures for event-based vision by comparing Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Vision Transformers (ViTs) under noisy sensing conditions. Using the GEN1 event-camera dataset, the study evaluates the performance of ResNet34 and ViT-B16 for vehicle classification, demonstrating the potential of event-based vision systems for deployment in dynamic applications such as autonomous vehicles and UAVs.

## Key Contribution
- Conducted a comprehensive comparison of ResNet34 and Vision Transformer (ViT-B16) for event-based vehicle classification.
- Fine-tuned both architectures on the GEN1 event-camera dataset and evaluated their performance under clean and simulated noisy conditions.
- Demonstrated that ResNet34 achieved the highest classification accuracy (88%), while ViT-B16 achieved 86% and exhibited strong robustness despite being pre-trained on a relatively smaller dataset.
- Investigated the impact of sensor noise on event-based vision models, providing insights into the robustness of CNN- and Transformer-based architectures.
- Highlighted the potential of event-based cameras and Vision Transformers for real-time perception in dynamic environments, including UAVs, autonomous vehicles, and intelligent robotic systems.
- Established a foundation for extending event-based computer vision techniques from ground vehicle classification to aerial perception and autonomous aviation applications.
