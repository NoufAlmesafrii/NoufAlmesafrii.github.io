This work investigates a hybrid renewable power system for electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), integrating photovoltaic (PV) panels with hydrogen fuel cells to enhance endurance and operational capability. By assigning hydrogen fuel cells to power the vertical flight motors and photovoltaic panels to supplement forward flight, the study evaluates how renewable energy technologies can overcome the limitations of conventional battery-powered UAVs.

## Key Contribution
- Developed and analyzed a hybrid propulsion architecture combining hydrogen fuel cells and photovoltaic (PV) panels for eVTOL UAVs.
- Evaluated the use of hydrogen fuel cells to extend hover and vertical flight performance, while utilizing solar energy to support forward flight operations.
- Demonstrated extended hover times of 35 minutes and 55 minutes using 3.5 L and 5 L hydrogen cylinders, respectively.
- Increased the UAV's operational capability by enabling 4 to 6 take-off and landing cycles, significantly improving mission flexibility.
- Investigated the potential of renewable energy integration to enhance flight endurance, payload capability, and overall energy efficiency for Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) platforms.
