This work investigates the integration of a hybrid hydrogen fuel cell power system into an electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) to improve flight endurance. By replacing the conventional battery-only architecture with a hybrid configuration—using hydrogen fuel cells during forward flight and lithium polymer batteries during take-off and landing—the study demonstrates a significant increase in operational endurance while maintaining the aircraft within its design constraints.

## Key Contribution
- Designed and evaluated a hybrid hydrogen fuel cell and lithium polymer battery power architecture for eVTOL UAVs.
- Performed system-level simulations on the Dronepoulis ESA-90 platform to assess power and endurance performance.
- Demonstrated an increase in flight endurance from 90 minutes to 272 minutes, representing more than a threefold improvement over the original battery-powered configuration.
- Investigated the effects of hydrogen storage pressure and cylinder size on flight endurance and overall system performance.
- Identified design trade-offs between hydrogen storage capacity, aircraft weight, and maximum take-off weight constraints for practical UAV deployment.
